We connect people.


At Operation: OneStep, our main goal is to level the playing field for those who werenâ€™t afforded the opportunity to be successful. We want to give kids a chance to look past their circumstances and be more than they think they can be. By connecting high school students to the nearest shelter, we try to make the process of volunteering easier and thus inspiring more to lend a hand. The creation of Operation: OneStep was inspired by the fact that trying to find somewhere to volunteer as a high school student was difficult and discouraging. Most volunteer opportunities were only mentioned by word of mouth and were unreliable. There was a gap in the market for something like OOS and as a result, it lowered the likelihood of students actually wanting to give back to their community. We hope that Operation: OneStep will create a pipeline between caring and devoted high school students to intelligent, disadvantaged children.

Tutors

All high school students are welcome, however, if you want to tutor in academics you must maintain a minimum 3.5 GPA. If you would you like to tutor in extracurricular activities, such as music, the academic requirement do not pertain to you.

Parents

We not only want to focus on the children in shelters, but also their families. The Jobs button allows the parents/ family members to easily access information about jobs hiring in their area.

Mentors

Unfortunately, most of the children in shelters do not have access to positive role models. Through our mentoring program we aim to match each and every child with an individual, who will postively impact their lives and future paths.
